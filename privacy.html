Privacy Policy for PG50

Last updated: July 4, 2026

PG50 ("the app") is designed so that almost everything happens on your device. This page explains exactly what data the app touches and why.

What PG50 collects

  • Your first name (optional): if you choose to sign in with Apple, PG50 asks only for your first name so it can personalize onboarding. This is stored locally on your device and is never sent to us or anyone else — PG50 has no servers.
  • A device-only usage counter: PG50 stores a small piece of data in your device's Keychain to keep track of your free-plan usage (e.g. how many palettes you've created). This value never leaves your device and contains no personal information.
  • Photos you choose to use: when you pick a photo to sample colors from, PG50 only sees the specific photo you select — it never has general access to your photo library. When you save a finished palette, PG50 asks permission to add that one image to your camera roll.

What PG50 does not do

  • PG50 does not have a server or database — nothing you do in the app is transmitted anywhere.
  • PG50 does not use analytics, tracking, or advertising SDKs of any kind.
  • PG50 does not share, sell, or transmit any data to third parties.

Subscriptions and payment

If you subscribe to PG50 Pro, your purchase is handled entirely by Apple through the App Store and StoreKit. PG50 never receives your payment details — only a confirmation from Apple that your subscription is active.

Your choices

You can delete all app data at any time by deleting the app from your device. Since PG50 stores everything locally, this permanently removes it.
